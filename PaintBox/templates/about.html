Paint Conversion Chart

Assembly

Cut the model out of the sprue and shave off excess materials with an exacto knife or using sprue cutters. Glue it together using super glue or plastic glue (ONLY if you’re using models that are plastic and that are receptive to the plastic glue, ex: Games Workshop models.)

Priming

Apply primer to the assembled models, whether by spraying it with a primer or applying it with a brush (I would suggest applying with a brush only if you’re working on a single model.

Painting

Get the paints and brushed of your choice and start painting! (Remember to thin your paints!)

Shading

Using a larger brush, spread a shade of your choice over the model, letting it pool naturally over the model, where ever it pools heavily, push it around with the brush. Remember, there’s no such thing as too much shade!

Highlighting

Brighten up parts of the model by highlighting edges with a lighter shade of paint.

Details

Paint eyes, energy effects, blood, rust, etc. Things that you DO NOT want to be affected by the shading.

Base

Decorate the base of your mode using special paint, real sand, cork, water effects, etc. Also do things like painting front/back arc at this step.
